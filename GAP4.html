Supporting Your Daughter

Don't wait for your kids to come to you with questions about their changing body. They might not, especially if they don’t know that it's OK to ask you about this sensitive topic. Talking about puberty isn’t a one-time conversation. Talk to your kids about the changes their bodies will go through as they grow. Some girls start puberty at 8 years old, and some boys do by 9. So you may need to start these talks earlier than you think. Discuss the physical and emotional changes that come with puberty before they begin.
